1. Land Preparation

Choose good, loose soil with proper drainage. Chili peppers require warm, well-drained soil to prevent waterlogging and plant damage. Clear the field of weeds and debris before planting.

2. Seed Selection

Select chili pepper varieties suitable for your climate. There are hot and mild types. Choose seeds resistant to pests and diseases for a better harvest.

3. Shuka Tarugu

A shuka iri a cikin layuka ko ramuka, a bar nisan kusan 30-50cm tsakanin tsire-tsire da 60-75cm tsakanin layuka. Ana iya fara shuka a cikin masaka sannan a dasa a waje bayan tsiro.

3. Planting Chili

Plant seeds in rows or holes, spacing about 30-50cm between plants and 60-75cm between rows. Seeds can be started in nurseries and transplanted outdoors after seedlings develop.

4. Fertilizer Application

Use fertilizers containing nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), especially NPK 15-15-15 or organic manure at planting and during growth to increase yield. Modern or animal manure fertilizers can be used.

5. Irrigation

Chili requires proper and regular watering, especially during dry periods. Water 2-3 times a week or use drip irrigation to conserve soil moisture without wasting water. Avoid overwatering to prevent plant damage.

6. Kare Tsire-Tsire daga Kwari da Cututtuka

6. Pest and Disease Control

Monitor for pests like aphids, spider mites, and diseases such as powdery mildew. Use appropriate pesticides and fungicides, and promptly remove infected plants to prevent spread.

7. Girbi da Ajiye Tarugu

Tarugu na shirya girbi bayan kwanaki 70 zuwa 90 daga shuka. A yanke tarugu lokacin da suka yi ja ko kore, a kula kada su yi tsufa a gona. A ajiye su a wuri mai sanyi da iska mai kyau don tsawaita rayuwa.

7. Harvesting and Storage

Chilies are ready to harvest 70 to 90 days after planting. Pick them when red or green, avoid over-maturity in the field. Store in a cool, well-ventilated place to extend shelf life.
